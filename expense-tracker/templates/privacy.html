Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 16, 2026

1. Data We Collect

When you create a Spendly account, we collect basic profile information such as your name, email address, and a hashed password. As you use the Service, we also store the expense entries you choose to record — amount, category, date, and any description you add.

We may collect limited technical information automatically, including your browser type, device, and approximate location derived from your IP address, to keep the Service secure and functional.

2. How We Use Your Data

Your data is used solely to provide and improve Spendly. That means displaying your expenses back to you, generating category breakdowns and monthly summaries, and maintaining the security of your account.

We do not use your financial data for advertising, and we do not sell or rent it to third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Data Storage

Your account and expense data are stored on secure servers with industry-standard encryption in transit and at rest. Passwords are never stored in plain text — they are hashed using a strong, one-way algorithm.

You may delete your account at any time, which will remove your profile and associated expense records from active systems. Residual copies in encrypted backups are purged on our standard rotation schedule.

4. Third Party Services

Spendly may rely on a small number of trusted third-party providers — for example, for hosting, email delivery, or error monitoring. These providers only receive the information they need to perform their function and are contractually required to protect it.

We do not share your individual expense records with any third party except where strictly required by law.

5. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, would like to access or delete your data, or want to raise a privacy concern, please reach out to us at privacy@spendly.example. We aim to respond to all requests within a reasonable timeframe.
